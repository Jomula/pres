Challenges, Potentials and Lessons of Open Data Aggregation

What is correspSearch?

correspSearch is a web service for the aggregation of open and freely accessible metadata from printed and digital editions of correspondences.

As a web service, correspSearch offers the aggregated metadata via an free API as Open Data.

Developed and hosted by Berlin Brandenburg Academy of Sciences and Humanities.

Awarded with the Rahtz Price for TEI Ingenuity 2018.

Correspondence Metadata Interchange Format (CMIF)

Developed by the TEI Correspondence SIG.

CMIF offers a way for a standardized interchange of correspondence metadata.

CMIF requires the usage of authority files for names and places in order to account for their ambiguity and linguistic heterogeneity.

CMIF Creator

Browser-based application for creating CMIF-files easily.

The CMIF Creator enables the creation of correspondence metadata without any prerequisite knowledge about XML/TEI or the CMI format.

Intended users are editors who are neither accustomed to the use of XML nor the tools of Digital Humanities.

The CMIF Creator will help to incorporate research “data“ from printend scholarly editions.

All entered and processed data is saved locally and thus stays within the control of the user at any time.

The CMIF Creator User Interface offers an opportunity, to acquire authority files data for names and places directly from GND and GeoNames via the lobid and GeoNames APIs.

The average time to process and enter the metadata of a single letter out of a printed letter edition is approximately 30 to 60 seconds, the search for authority file IDs included.

That means it takes about a half working day to process an average volume of letters (250 - 400 letters).

On-the-fly validation assures users of the validity of their data.

Errors can be traced to their occurrence before the final export to CMIF-XML.

csLink

csLink is a widget for websites that can be implemented and included in existing digital editions of correspondence.

Links letters of the same correspondents in the same period in other scholarly editions by using the correspSearch API.

csLink is possible due to our open data policy.

csLink situate a letter in a single edition within the wider context of correspondence and communication.

It establishes a “network of letters”, displaying the correspondence network of a certain person and reaching beyond the scope of a single edition of letters.

Ongoing development of additional ways to comprehensively visualize the aggregated data - starting in csLink.

Conclusion

The Open Data approach has proven successful.

The Open Data policy allows not only aggregation, but also unlimited use of data beyond project boundaries.

With around 54,000 aggregated data records, correspSearch represents an important hub for correspondence metadata.

Applications like the CMIF Creator close the gap between traditional scholarly editing and DH infrastructure.

Applications like csLink provide an immediate visibility of the benefits of data sharing.

Challenge: To reach “traditional” editors and encourage them to provide the data. More work is needed in the various scholarly communities.
